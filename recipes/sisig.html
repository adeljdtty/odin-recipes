Sisig

Description

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish that originated in the province of Pampanga.

It is typically made from parts of a pig's head such as the cheeks, ears, and snout, although other cuts of meat such as chicken or fish can also be used.

Ingredients

  • pork belly
  • garlic
  • onion
  • chili peppers
  • soy sauce
  • vinegar
  • calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • mayonnaise
  • egg

Steps

This are the steps in making Sisig.

  1. Cut the pork into small pieces, place them in a pot, and add enough water to cover the meat, then add salt and pepper.
  2. Bring the pot to a boil, and simmer until the pork is tender. Drain the pork and set aside.
  3. Preheat a grill, then grill the boiled pork pieces until they are crispy and lightly charred. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions, garlic, and chili peppers, and saut√© until the onions are translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
  5. Add the grilled pork to the pan with the onions, garlic, and chili peppers, and mix well.
  6. Pour soy sauce and vinegar over the pork mixture, and continue to cook and stir until the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Transfer the sisig to a sizzling plate, garnish with sliced chili peppers and calamansi juice.
  8. Add mayonnaise and raw egg on top of the sisig. Then siszle and serve.
